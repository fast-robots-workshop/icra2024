Workshop on Fast Robots @ ICRA2024

Robots today often navigate or manipulate slowly and intermittently, relying on bursts of computation for planning. This is in contrast to humans who routinely use fast and dynamic motions such as vaulting power cords over chairs when vacuuming, flinging garments to flatten before folding, lofting sheets across beds, jumping across obstacles, and running for fun or to reach a destination on time. Dynamic motions can speed task completion and manipulate objects out of reach,but they require careful integration of grasp planning, motion planning, and time-parameterization, lifting quasi-static assumptions, and strong safety considerations.

The objectives of the workshop would be to understand how we can get dynamic robots that operate faster and reliably, and we wish for the workshop scope to contain both manipulation and mobility (or navigation). By including both manipulation and mobility, we aim to broaden the appeal of the workshop and also explore and discuss similarities and differences between the two. By doing so we hope to open the doors to robots that operate faster in today’s world.

The workshop will be held in hybrid mode

Content

Topics

What is needed in order to get robots that can move and manipulate faster, ideally without sacrificing reliability and safety? What are the similarities and differences between methods used for dynamic manipulation versus dynamic mobility, and how can the techniques be merged together to achieve better performance? We aim to discuss and debate how to answer these questions during the workshop. Hence, the scope of the workshop focuses primarily on (but is not limited to) the following topics:

  • Robot systems that are fast (high speed), agile (can change directions quickly), dynamic, etc.
  • Methods to allow robots to move faster given fixed existing hardware
  • Hardware and/or mechanical design for fast robots
  • Agility and stability of robots operating at high speeds
  • The intersection of fast manipulation and mobility for “whole body” manipulation
  • Integration and synchronization of grasp planning, motion planning, and time parameterization
  • Machine learning to help fast and dynamic robots generalize
  • Safety in unstructured situations with humans or other robots nearby
  • Application-specific challenges: fast bin-picking, fast assembly, fast delivery robots, tossing objects, catching objects, etc.
